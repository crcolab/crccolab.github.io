About CRC

CRC (Cyborg Resilience Co-lab) was founded in July 2025 as a collaborative initiative bringing together researchers, hackers, digital rights activists, and artists to explore digital resilience under contemporary conditions in which war, disaster, and technological risk increasingly overlap.

Rather than operating as a conventional research center, CRC functions as a loosely connected co-lab that bridges research and practice. Through ethnographic research, technical experimentation, artistic practice, workshops, and public events, we investigate decentralized communication, network infrastructures, drone technologies, and other emerging technologies. Together, we examine how posthuman warfare is imagined, enacted, and contested across military, civic, and digital domains.

Why "Posthuman Warfare"?

Contemporary conflicts increasingly unfold through communication networks, algorithms, data infrastructures, drones, and other interconnected technologies that blur the boundaries between war and peace, humans and machines, and the battlefield and everyday life. Posthuman warfare offers a way of understanding these entanglements and how violence and power are increasingly enacted through humans, machines, algorithms, and infrastructures.

Partner

OpenFun

Founded in 2023, OpenFun is a civic-technology company whose name combines “Open” and “Fun.” It builds dependable digital infrastructure for open data, public participation, and informed decision-making, including the LawTrace legislative tracker and an AI-assisted parliamentary transcript system. CRC and OpenFun received support from the TWNIC Internet Community Program in 2026.
